# FIELD BRIEF PRODUCT VISION V2

## Mission

FieldBrief exists to help law enforcement officers make legally sound, department-compliant decisions before, during, and after every shift.

Rather than forcing officers to search through statutes, case law, policy manuals, training bulletins, and emails, FieldBrief delivers the information that matters most, when it matters most.

Our long-term mission is to become the operational legal briefing platform trusted by law enforcement agencies.

---

# Vision

Every officer should begin every shift knowing:

* What changed.
* Why it matters.
* What action, if any, they should take.

Every supervisor should know their agency is operating with current legal information and department guidance.

FieldBrief exists to bridge the gap between legal information, department policy, and real-world decision making.

---

# Core Philosophy

An officer should never have to wonder:

* Did the law change?
* Did my department policy change?
* What am I expected to know today?

FieldBrief answers those questions before the officer leaves briefing.

---

# The Five Pillars

## 1. Officer Experience

Designed for patrol officers.

Simple.

Fast.

Minimal.

Every feature should answer one question:

**"What do I need to know before my shift?"**

Modules include:

* Daily Brief
* Search
* Saved Briefs
* Notifications
* Profile

---

## 2. Operations Center

Designed for supervisors, legal advisors, administrators, trainers, and command staff.

Every feature should answer one question:

**"What needs my attention today?"**

Modules include:

* Operations Dashboard
* Operational Brief Management
* Review Queue
* Trusted Sources
* Department Policies
* Users
* Agencies
* Analytics
* Settings

---

## 3. AI Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence is an assistant.

It is never the decision maker.

AI responsibilities include:

* Reading court opinions
* Reading legislation
* Reading policy updates
* Drafting operational briefs
* Suggesting officer takeaways
* Estimating operational impact
* Categorizing updates
* Assigning confidence scores
* Detecting duplicate updates

Every AI-generated brief must be reviewed and approved before publication.

---

## 4. Agency Knowledge

This is FieldBrief's defining feature.

Every officer should receive two clearly separated answers.

### Legal Standard

What federal and state law requires.

### Department Policy

What the officer's own agency requires.

These answers may not always be identical.

FieldBrief should clearly distinguish between legal requirements and agency policy so officers understand both what is legally permitted and what their agency expects.

---

## 5. Trust & Verification

Trust is FieldBrief's most valuable feature.

Every legal brief should clearly identify:

* The official source
* The publication date
* The effective date
* Whether AI assisted in drafting
* Whether a human reviewer approved it
* The agency or organization responsible for publication

FieldBrief should never ask officers to trust the software.

FieldBrief should help officers trust the source.

* United States Supreme Court
* Federal Circuit Courts
* State Supreme Courts
* State Legislatures
* Attorneys General
* POST Boards
* Department Policies
* Agency Training Bulletins

Every published brief should reference an official source.
